Intro

Character Creation

The only change in character creation is that some Races cannot play certain classes. This mainly represents the fact some races cannot use biotic powers. General character creation works as follows:

  1. Choose a race & feats (if applicable)
  2. Choose a class available to that race.
  3. Choose your proficiencies.
  4. Roll for your Hit Points and Shield Points.
  5. Roll and assign your ability scores.
  6. Choose 2 starting weapons.
  7. Create a back story.

General Changes & Additions

Features

"Feature" is just the umbrella term used for Spells, Tech Powers, and 5e class features.

In Mass Effect, spells and tech powers have advanced versions of themselves. At certain levels, you can either choose to learn new spells and powers or advance ones you've already learned. For example, when you see Select (2:4) spells. The first number indicates the number of new spells you can learn or advance. In this example, you can either learn 2 new spells, advance 2 spells, or learn a spell and advance a spell. The second number indicates the max level of the spell you can learn or advance. In this example, you can learn or advance spells of level 4 or less, including cantrips. Though you can learn new catrips this way, you cannot know more cantrips than allowed by your level (indicated on you class sheet). You cannot select and advance a spell in the same turn.

Tech powers are all considered level 1; thus, there is only a single number in the parenthesis, indicating the number of powers you can learn or advance.

Feats

The available feats from the 5e PHB are: Alert, Athlete, Actor, Biotic Slayer*, Charger, Durable, Elemental Adept, Grappler, Heavily Armored, Heavily Armored, Heavy Armor Master, Inspiring Leader, Keen Mind, Lightly Armored, Linguist, Lucky, Medium Armor Master, Mobile, Moderately Armored, Observant, Resilient, Savage Attacker, Sentinel, Sharpshooter, Skilled, Skulker, Tavern Brawler, Tough, Weapon Master**

  • * - see Mage Slayer
  • ** - Instead of gain proficiency with 4 weapons, you gain proficiency with 2 ranged weapons

Paragon & Renegade Points

These are special proficiency bonuses that can be applied to certain skill checks. For example, you might try get information out of another character. If you choose to intimidate the target with a threat, the GM would ask for an Intimidation check. You can ask, "Does my Renegade bonus apply?". In this case, it should. You might choose to Persuade the character for information, to which the Paragon bonus would apply. It's your responsibility to ask the GM about adding the P/R bonus, but the GM can proactively ask for a check with the P/R bonus added.

Paragon and Renegade is earned as you play. It can be rewarded by the GM based on actions you take during the campaign. It's a permanent bonus. You get a +1 bonus for every 10 Paragon and Renegade points. The GM can adjust the ratio if there aren't a lot of opportunities to gain points. The idea is that you shouldn't ever get more than ~ a +6 bonus. For a long campaign, this amounts to 60 points, giving the GM many opportunities to reward points. However, use a 1:5 ratio for shot campaigns or if you don't have an many situations which would provide points.

Distance

I use meters instead of feet. Why? Because this is set in the future and it's sci-fi and using metric just seems to make more sense. The general rule of thumb is that 3ft = 1m.

Armor

Armor proficiency is based on race not class.

Instead of the different types of light, medium, and heavy armor, (studded, chain mail, etc.) there are only the 3 major types (light, medium, and heavy). Armor does take away from your carry capacity.

Type Armor Class (AC) Stealth Weight
Light 11 + Dex modifier - 1
Medium 14 + Dex modifier (max 2) - 2
Heavy 17 Disadvantage 3

There are plenty of armor bonuses in Mass Effect. I've not created a list (yet?), but you and your GM should be creative about armor bonuses.
